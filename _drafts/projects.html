Quantitative MRI Contrast Project

Pineapple juice was a negative contrast agent we give to participant before their MRI scans. Because of its high concentration of Manganese, the gastrointestinal tract filled with pineapple juice will show dark and hence increase the visibility of other structures. The main purpose this research project though, instead of focusing on visual improvement on images, was to investigate whether it affect other biomarkers’ reliability. I use libraries such as pandas, bumpy, matplotlib and scipy to conduct analysis. One of the major analysis methodology I learned during this project is the “Bland Altman” analysis. It is a statistical method relating mean and differences for 2 measurements with scatter plots visual representation. Often it is used to compare whether one newly developed measurement method is in agreement with a relatively more established methods. It is also use to judge measurement precision i.e. repeatability and reproducibility for the same measurement method.

To me this is one of the best scientific project I was assigned to. Upon presenting to my colleagues, not only did I showed improved skilling in coding but I also provided the answer that was demanded by many of our clients. The associated experience of collaborating with SWE team also make me realise my contribution in a scalable business. 👩🏼‍💻
